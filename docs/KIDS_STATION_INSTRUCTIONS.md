# Morse Station Instructions For Students

This guide is for students using Pappy's Internet Telegraph.

Printable one-page version: [Pappy's Operators Handout](KIDS_QUICK_START_HANDOUT.pdf)

## What This Station Does

The station helps you learn Morse code by using:

- your eyes
- your ears
- your hand on the telegraph key
- short practice missions

Morse code uses short signals and long signals:

```text
dit = .
dah = -
```

## Main Rule

Practice one step at a time.

Do not worry about knowing all the letters yet. The station will give you a small group of letters, help you practice them, and then unlock more when you are ready.

## Pick Your Name

When the station starts:

1. Tap your name.
2. If you do not see your name, ask Pappy or an adult.
3. After you pick your name, go to Daily.

Your progress is saved under your own name.

## Daily

Daily is your mission screen.

It shows:

- what letters you know
- what you are learning now
- what to practice next
- how much of today's mission is done
- how much practice time you have put in today

Start with the Next Step on Daily.

When you finish a practice area, tap Daily again to see what to do next.

## Learn

Learn is for new letters.

You will see:

- the letter
- the Morse code
- the sound
- the light

Try to key the code with the telegraph key.

New letters stay in Learn first. They do not show up in Send, Read, Listen, or Echo until you practice them enough.

## Send

Send shows you a letter.

Your job:

```text
key the Morse code for that letter
```

If you get it right, the station moves to another letter.

If you miss it, clear and try the same letter again.

## Read

Read shows you Morse code.

Your job:

```text
choose the letter that matches the code
```

This helps your eyes learn the Morse patterns.

## Listen

Listen plays Morse code.

Your job:

```text
listen and choose the letter
```

You can tap Play Again if you need to hear it again.

Listen is for hearing. You do not need to use the key in Listen.

## Echo

Echo plays Morse code first.

Your job:

```text
hear it, then key it back
```

This helps your ears and hand work together.

## Words

Words unlock after you learn enough letters.

Your job:

```text
see the word, listen to the word, then key the whole word
```

If you get it right, the screen flashes.

If you miss it:

1. Tap Clear.
2. Try the word again.

Words only use letters you have already learned.

## Signal Sprint

Signal Sprint is a bonus round after Daily is done.

Your job:

```text
key as many active letters as you can
```

Try for good accuracy and a strong streak.

## How New Letters Unlock

New letters unlock when you show you are ready.

The station checks:

- your current letters are strong
- you learned the new letters in Learn
- you had a short break
- you practiced some Words after Words unlock

Sometimes the station may say:

```text
Practice Words
Take A Break
Come Back Tomorrow
```

That is not a failure. It means your brain is getting time to remember the new code.

## Good Practice Habits

- Keep your hand on the key during practice.
- Listen before you tap.
- Make dashes longer than dots.
- Pause between letters.
- Use Clear when you want to try again.
- Use Daily to know what to do next.
- Stop before you get frustrated.
- Notice your practice time. More steady practice helps your Morse get stronger.

## Grit Badges

The station also rewards effort.

Examples:

- Focused Practice: you gave active practice time.
- Try Again Champ: you missed one, tried again, and kept going.

These badges mean you are learning how to practice, not just getting answers right.

## What To Do If Something Seems Wrong

Ask an adult if:

- no sound plays
- the light does not flash
- the key does not show anything
- your name is missing
- the screen is stuck
- you are not sure what to practice next

Most problems can be fixed by going back to Daily or restarting the station.
